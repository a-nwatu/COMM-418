Continuing the analysis with the Gen-Z Slang Tracker Dataset, the image above shows the distribution of slang usage across social media platforms (Discord, Instagram, Reddit, TikTok, Twitch, Twitter, and YouTube), by slang sentiment (negative, positive, neutral). This graph also tracks the amount of terms used, as we can see, TikTok has the largest amount of slang use on its platform compared to other platforms. While TikTok is leading in high slang usage, social platforms such as Twitter and Instagram following in high usage. While platforms such as Discord and Twitch underperform. One explanation for this gap in usage could be from the user interface and purpose of the various social media platforms. Discord and Twitch are known for having experiences that on long-term surveillance and watching, compared to platforms like Instagram and TikTok where smaller forms of enagement such as "likes" and leaving comments on videos mean a lot to the algorithm. There are more opportunities for uses to share their thoughts on platforms that push short-term content onto users compared to platforms that push long-term content. 

Outside of being able to tell slang usage across platforms,. The graph also informs us of the usage of slang within each of the sentiment categories; negative, neutral, and positive. The stacked-bar graph shows the proportionality of sentiments used across each platform. The distribution of the positive sentiments (identified by the green bars) show us that platforms such as Instagram and YouTube are more likely to use slang that has a positive connotation compared to a negative connotation. Platforms like Twitch and Discord have a more equal distribution of sentiments in its users' slang usage.
