The image above is a graph showing the relationship between slang sentiment (negative, neutral, positive) by slang category (approval, insult, identity, humor, appearance, reaction, emotion, emphasis, behavior, dating, attraction, manipulation, description, meme, exposure, social, and food). The distribution shows us that the top three categories for the slang category are approval, insult, and identity. Slang that's used to express approval is overall positive compared to identity that is used more neutrally. Another interesting insight is that slang that indicates manipulation is overwhelmingly negative.

The Gen Z Slang dataset gives us insight on the types of sentiments that we express online, more often than not, our reactions are values of approval or denial. For example, categories such such insult, manipulation, emphasis, or manipulation can be indicators of negative or coercive behavior online. While categories such as approval, reaction, humor, or attraction can be words used to encourage positive behavior online. All slang categories give us insight on how language is used online. Further inquiry would have us wonder what category of words are used on which social platforms; do we find more slang words that have more of a positive sentiment on TikTok or Reddit? What does that tell us about that social media platform's culture or how users engage on that site?
