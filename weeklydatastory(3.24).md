<h4> WIRED: Surveillance and ICE  Are Driving Patients Away From Medial Care, Report Warns</h4>

<p>
WIRED's article dives into data privacy and protection concerns in the face of immmigration enforcement in hostipals. WIRED cites research from the Electronic Privacy Information Center (EPIC), a non-profit public research advocacy center, on how private corporations data sharing practices might have violated the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

Data sharing practices are a privacy concern that has an ethical question, "What data should the government or private companies be able to collect from citizens, and to what extend should they be able to use it." EPIC argues that there's a growing "health privacy crisis" occuring and it's deterring patients from seeking care. HIPAA is supposed to protect the public from invasive questions that might make them identifiable. 

ICE has been known to surveil sensitive locations such as emergency rooms, waiting rooms and hospital lobbies. The Department of Homeland Security has gently advice immigration places from avoiding enforcement practices in places such as schools, religious institutions and medical/mental care facilities. In the event where ICE has access to identifiable information, there's no telling what place they might detain someone;potentially putting multiple bystanders in harms way. Data selling and sharing, as EPIC points out, is increasingly becoming a risk for people them and causing a chilling effect--less and less people feel comfortable to enter public spaces or get the help they need. 

ICE being able to not only have access to that data, but to use that data unconsensually is a problem. WIRED cites how 404 Media, an independent journalistic organization that focusing on technology's effect on the world, found that ICE agents have gained acces to ISO ClaimSearch, a private insurance and medical database and using it to identify people for deportation. Legally, ICE should not be able to do that, however the United States has not modernized its regulations to compete with data privacy breaches.

WIRED presents an expansion on what considerations are needed for protecting citizens and their data outside of the Big Tech space.

Article Link: https://www.wired.com/story/surveillance-and-ice-are-driving-patients-away-from-medical-care-report-warns/
